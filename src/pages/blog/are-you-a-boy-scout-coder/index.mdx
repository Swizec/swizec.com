> The Boy Scouts have a rule: "Always leave the campground cleaner than you found it." If you find a mess on the ground, you clean it up regardless of who might have made the mess. /../ the original form by Robert Stephenson Smyth Baden-Powell, was "Try and leave this world a little better than you found it." What if we followed a similar rule in our code: "Always check a module in cleaner than when you checked it out." No matter who the original author was, what if we always made some effort, no matter how small, to improve the module. What would be the result? by [Uncle Bob](http://programmer.97things.oreilly.com/wiki/index.php/Uncle_Bob "Uncle Bob") at [O'Reilly commons](http://programmer.97things.oreilly.com/wiki/index.php/The_Boy_Scout_Rule)
